# Noridoc: ui/organisms

Path: @/ui/organisms

### Overview

- The third presentational layer of the Shemá design system: stateful compositions that consume @/domain state via props/hooks and own the interaction-critical surfaces the listener touches. This folder ships the **necklace**, the **conversation stage**, the **storyteller guide** and the **seam modal**, all exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts.
- The **necklace** (`ui/organisms/necklace`, ENG-220) is the hero "colar" (redesign §6) that renders the whole story as a wrapped field of beads: the full bead-state vocabulary (palette-tinted segment beads, square end beads for locked scene ends, a soft selection band with emphasized edge beads, a dim + dashed scene-band over the active-scene window in Segmentação) plus its own click model, hover-edge preview and 60 fps playback lighting.
- The **conversation stage** (`ui/organisms/conversation-stage`, ENG-221) is the Mapeamento (Conversa) surface (redesign §6.6), and the **storyteller guide** (`ui/organisms/storyteller-guide`, ENG-221) is the warm human figure it seats on the left (§6.6, §9.7). Both are purely presentational — props in, callbacks out.
- The **connection gate** (`ui/organisms/connection-gate`, ENG-224) is the online-only editing shield (PRD §7.3/§13): `ConnectionGate({ online, children })` renders its children always mounted and, when offline, adds a PT-BR `role=status` banner plus a blocking overlay over the editing surface. It is a pure organism (no adapters — `online` arrives resolved by the shell), and is deliberately NOT exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts (the barrel was out of ENG-224's scope); @/ui/app imports it by direct path.
- The **seam modal** (`ui/organisms/seam-modal`, ENG-228) is the border-crossing dialog (PRD v2 §8.6; redesign §6.5, prototype "1g — a costura desliza"): a full-olive panel showing a small stretch of the cord with today's border and the proposed new one, offering exactly the choices the domain classified. The Segmentação station will open it when a phrase selection crosses the scene border (wiring lands with ENG-237).
- Unlike @/ui/atoms and @/ui/molecules, organisms are allowed to import @/domain **types** and hold interaction state; they still never import adapters.

### How it fits into the larger codebase

- Sits between @/ui/molecules and the wiring layers (@/ui/pages, @/ui/templates, @/ui/app). Consumers import from the public barrel @/ui/organisms/index.ts; sibling organisms import each other by direct path (never the barrel) to avoid cycles — the same rule atoms and molecules follow.
- The dependency direction is enforced mechanically by @/.dependency-cruiser.cjs, not convention: organisms may import @/domain (types only), @/ui/tokens (`PaletteEntry`) and the @/ui/atoms / @/ui/molecules barrels; the ban on adapter imports is a required CI check.
- **Selection decisions stay in the domain.** The necklace reports a raw bead index through callbacks (`onBeadPointerDown`, `onEdgeHover`, `onHeadTap`) and never decides what a click means — the click model that turns a bead into a selection lives in @/domain/selection.ts. The organism is the pointer-to-index transducer plus the renderer; the page wires the domain decision back in as new props.
- `Span` (the `{ s, e }` bead range) is the shared coordinate between this organism and @/domain — segments, selection, the active-scene window and locked-end beads all arrive as bead indices, matching the "bead indices are the universal coordinate" invariant in @/domain.
- **The seam modal is the interpreter surface for the effects-as-data `BorderOffer`** from @/domain/seam.ts (see @/domain/docs.md): `classifyBorderMove` returns the offer as a pure value and the modal renders EXACTLY the option set that value encodes — `offer.kind` picks the variant (simple / escalation / two-productive) and `offer.canMove` gates "Mover mesmo assim". The modal never computes thresholds; the station will wire the three callbacks back into `moveBorder`/`reanchorFrase`/back-to-Triagem.

ui/tokens ──▶ ui/atoms ──▶ ui/molecules ──▶ ui/organisms ──▶ templates/pages/app
(PaletteEntry) (Pearl…)     (rows, bands)    (this folder:     (adapter wiring +

### Core Implementation

- **Geometry is a pure port** in @/ui/organisms/necklace/geometry.ts — a 1:1 translation of the reference `renderCord`/`drawBand`/`beadAtXY` (see @/docs/reference/index.html). It has zero DOM/framework imports: `resolveWindow` (active scene ± `max(3, round(2/beadSec))` margin, or the whole story when no scene is active), `beadsPerRow` (from container width), `beadPosition` (absolute left/top of a bead), `beadAtXY` (pointer coords → clamped bead index), and `bandRects` (one rectangle per row when a band wraps across the row break). The pure math is unit-tested independently of the component.
- **Imperative island for playback lighting** (@/docs/architecture.md §6): the bead field is a `React.memo` component that receives ONLY structural props — `playbackHead` is deliberately excluded, so feeding a new head does NOT re-render bead elements. A `useLayoutEffect` toggles a `data-play` attribute (`played`/`head`) on bead wrappers via the container ref. DOM node identity stays stable across head changes, so per-frame updates never pass through React state or reconciliation.
- **One delegated native pointer handler**: a single `pointerdown`/`pointermove`/`pointerleave` listener is attached with `addEventListener` on the container inside a mount-once `useLayoutEffect` (NOT React's `onPointerDown`, which delegates at the document root). Pointer → bead is pure geometry (`beadAtXY` over `getBoundingClientRect`), clamped to the render window.
- **Ref-mirror for live props**: because the listeners mount once, they read the latest props through `ixRef`, written inside a layout effect (NOT during render — the `react-hooks/refs` rule forbids ref mutation in render). This keeps the handlers current without re-attaching them.
- **Head-tap precedence** (redesign §6.2, "the glowing head pauses"): when the tapped bead equals the glowing `playbackHead`, `onHeadTap` fires instead of `onBeadPointerDown`.
- The `window` prop is the ACTIVE SCENE span; the organism derives the render window internally, dims the margin beads (`state='dim'`) and draws the dashed scene-band over the scene. `transportOnly` (Escuta/review) suppresses all selection affordances.
- **The conversation stage is a props-driven state machine** (@/ui/organisms/conversation-stage): a full-bleed deep-olive stage with the @/ui/organisms/storyteller-guide on the left and, on the right, the scripted question via the `QuestionCard` molecule (Merriweather text + wordless facilitator role marker + the conditional "Ouvir a pergunta" button, shown only when `onSpeakQuestion` is supplied). The recorder area switches on `recorderState` (`idle` → mic button / `recording` → live `WaveformBar` waveform driven by the `levels` prop + "Parar" / `recorded` → "ouvir" + "de novo"), emitting `onRecord`/`onStop`/`onPlay`/`onRerecord`. An optional `typedAnswer` channel and prev/next nav (`onPrev`/`onNext`) round it out. No MediaRecorder lives here — that state machine lives in the adapters layer and is wired by the station page (ENG-249).
- **The seam modal is purely presentational and controlled** (@/ui/organisms/seam-modal): props are the `BorderOffer | null` (type-only @/domain import; `null` renders nothing), the scene and its immediate neighbor as `{ span, tint }` cord data (`SeamCordSide`; `null` neighbor = the border only stretches the scene), plus the outcome callbacks `onMove`/`onReanchor`/`onGoTriagem`. A small pure strip model windows a few beads around the seam, clamps to the given spans and zones each bead as scene / overshoot / neighbor; the overshoot beads get a light core over the scene tint and the cream selection halo from prototype 1g.
- **First Radix primitive in the codebase** (`@radix-ui/react-dialog`; policy decided 2026-07-09): Radix contributes BEHAVIOR only — focus trap, ESC, `aria-modal`, scroll lock — while every visual stays 100% Shemá tokens via the usual `.cds-*` CSS. ESC and overlay click both funnel through Radix `onOpenChange(false)` → `onReanchor`, the safe default that only discards the selection (§9.5: guide, never punish).
- **Seam-slide preview is static-first**: both markers — "borda de hoje" (dashed) and "borda nova" (ember-glow) — always render, so the before/after states are visible without motion; the slide animation of the new border lives only inside `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`, CSS-source-asserted in tests exactly like the necklace. Marker order mirrors the crossing side (`crossEnd` vs `crossStart`).
- **The "thread of beads" progress row** at the bottom reuses the `BeadRow` molecule — one Pearl per question, `head` for `progress.current`, `lit` for each answered index, `unplayed` otherwise. Per PRD §9.2 it shows zero digits: the current bead beats the answered state, and no counter/total ever renders as text.
- **The storyteller guide introduces the component-level variant-glob upgrade pattern** (@/docs/architecture.md §4). Its `index.tsx` reads `import.meta.glob('./variants/*.tsx', { eager: true })` and the pure `pickVariantPath()` helper in @/ui/organisms/storyteller-guide/variant.ts selects a variant by file basename — preferring `animated` over `static`. This issue ships only `variants/static.tsx` (a warm human figure — head, face, gentle smile — drawn in SVG/CSS, deliberately NOT abstract/geometric); ENG-232 will add `variants/animated.tsx` (bob/blink/lip-sync) WITHOUT editing any existing file. This mirrors the shell's three `import.meta.glob` add-a-file registries, scoped inside a single component. The shared `GuideVariantProps` (`speaking`, `size`) let the static variant ignore `speaking` while the future animated one drives lip-sync from it.

### Things to Know

- @/ui/organisms/necklace/minimalism.test.tsx is the layer-level guard for PRD v2 §9.2 listener minimalism, mirroring the atoms/molecules guards: the rendered necklace may show no digit as visible text, `aria-label` or `title`. IDs and counters never appear on this listener-facing surface.
- **Test split by file suffix** (@/vitest.config.ts): `*.test.tsx` runs in jsdom (`necklace.test.tsx` asserts the render vocabulary; `geometry.test.ts` covers the pure math), while `*.browser.test.tsx` runs in real Chromium — required here because jsdom has zeroed layout and no real hit-testing. There is **no `vitest-browser-react`** in the repo: browser tests drive the component with raw `createRoot`/`flushSync` and native `dispatchEvent(new PointerEvent(...))` for coordinate hit-testing.
- The 280 ms hover-edge dwell (a single edge preview fires only after the pointer rests near a selection edge) is tested with `vi.useFakeTimers()` + `advanceTimersByTimeAsync`; the single-listener claim is verified by spying on `addEventListener`. Touch pointers skip hover entirely.
- `ResizeObserver` is feature-detected — absent in jsdom, where the one synchronous width measure suffices; in the browser it re-measures on resize.
- Pearl (@/ui/atoms) stays non-clickable: the organism owns all pointer handling, matching the necklace performance pattern described in @/ui/docs.md. The scene-end square, dim margin and selection edge-ring are all Pearl `data-*` states driven from `computeField`.
- **The recorder-flow interaction test is mandatory** and lives in `conversation-stage.browser.test.tsx` (real Chromium, raw `createRoot`/`flushSync` + native click), because the conversation recorder is one of CLAUDE.md's three interaction-critical surfaces; a companion `conversation-stage.test.tsx` runs in jsdom for the structural assertions. The seam modal follows the same split — its focus trap is only provable in Chromium (jsdom has no native Tab navigation), so its mandatory interaction test lives in the browser suite too.
- **The seam modal deliberately does NOT render the domain's `question`/`warning` strings**: those carry digits (the WHY_DELTA copy in `BORDER_COPY`, @/domain/seam.ts), and this is a listener-facing surface (§9.2). It shows the redesign headline "A frase passou da borda da cena.", the PRD button copy as local constants, and the consequence line only for the `simple` variant; `delta`/`thr` stay internal. A minimalism test sweeps every offer variant for leaked digits in text, `aria-label` and `title`.
- **Radix Dialog gotchas recorded for future tests**: `Dialog.Portal` renders to `document.body`, so tests query via `screen`/`document`, never the render container. The modal Dialog defers pointer-down-outside dismissal to the subsequent `click` event (`deferPointerDownOutside`), so outside-dismiss tests must fire pointerdown AND click — and the outside listener attaches in a `setTimeout(0)`, so give the tick first.
- **Context override, not a new variant**: inside the olive panel the Button atom's ghost variant (olive-on-cream by default) is recolored to a cream outline by a CSS rule scoped under `.cds-seam-modal` — a look-layer override per prototype 1g, leaving the @/ui/atoms Button untouched.
- **prefers-reduced-motion is honored throughout**: every decorative animation (the idle mic pulse in the conversation stage, the seam-slide in the seam modal) is gated under `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`, and the static storyteller guide carries no motion at all.
- **Sibling organisms import each other by direct path, never the barrel** — the conversation stage pulls the storyteller guide from `../storyteller-guide` (its folder `index.tsx` entry), keeping the variant-glob resolution encapsulated and avoiding a cycle through @/ui/organisms/index.ts.
- **The connection gate only shows the affordance; the actual freeze lives elsewhere.** Its overlay covers the editing surface but keeps children mounted so state is preserved, and the traveling player (@/ui/app/player-slot.tsx) sits in its own layer outside the overlay so playback survives an outage. The mutation block itself is the `apply` no-op in @/ui/state (see @/ui/state/docs.md), fed by the same offline signal.
