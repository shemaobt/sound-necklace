# Noridoc: ui/organisms

Path: @/ui/organisms

### Overview

- The third presentational layer of the Shemá design system: stateful compositions that consume @/domain state via props/hooks and own the interaction-critical surfaces the listener touches. This folder ships the **necklace**, the **conversation stage**, the **storyteller guide** and the **seam modal** (exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts) plus the out-of-barrel organisms — the shell-only **connection gate** and **tutorial popup** (consumed by @/ui/app via direct path) and the two Triagem organisms (ENG-225): the **Triagem picker** and the facilitator-only **coverage drawer** (consumed by the Triagem station via direct path until ENG-236 adds the export).
- The **necklace** (`ui/organisms/necklace`, ENG-220) is the hero "colar" (redesign §6) that renders the whole story as a wrapped field of beads: the full bead-state vocabulary (palette-tinted segment beads, square end beads for locked scene ends, a soft selection band with emphasized edge beads, a dim + dashed scene-band over the active-scene window in Segmentação) plus its own click model, hover-edge preview and 60 fps playback lighting.
- The **conversation stage** (`ui/organisms/conversation-stage`, ENG-221) is the Mapeamento (Conversa) surface (redesign §6.6), and the **storyteller guide** (`ui/organisms/storyteller-guide`, ENG-221) is the warm human figure it seats on the left (§6.6, §9.7). Both are purely presentational — props in, callbacks out.
- The **connection gate** (`ui/organisms/connection-gate`, ENG-224) is the online-only editing shield (PRD §7.3/§13): `ConnectionGate({ online, children })` renders its children always mounted and, when offline, adds a PT-BR `role=status` banner plus a blocking overlay over the editing surface. It is a pure organism (no adapters — `online` arrives resolved by the shell).
- The **seam modal** (`ui/organisms/seam-modal`, ENG-228) is the border-crossing dialog (PRD v2 §8.6; redesign §6.5, prototype "1g — a costura desliza"): a full-olive panel showing a small stretch of the cord with today's border and the proposed new one, offering exactly the choices the domain classified. The Segmentação station will open it when a phrase selection crosses the scene border (wiring lands with ENG-237).
- The **tutorial popup** (`ui/organisms/tutorial-popup`, ENG-231) is the facilitator's per-station tips chrome (PRD v2 §7.5): a cream card of one short PT-BR tip per station, anchored to a round "?" trigger in the shell's bottom-right overlay layer. It is mounted only by the shell addon @/ui/app/addons/tutorial.tsx (never by a station page); its dismissal semantics and localStorage key are documented in @/ui/organisms/tutorial-popup/README.md.
- The **Triagem picker** (`ui/organisms/triagem-picker`, ENG-225, PRD v2 §8.5) is the scene-kind chooser: a "Mais comuns" grid, a by-theme disclosure covering every kind, an always-visible dashed "Nenhum se encaixa" card, an accent-insensitive text filter, and a confidence step that replaces the grid once a kind is chosen. The **coverage drawer** (`ui/organisms/coverage-drawer`, "Cobertura · só facilitadora") is a right-side olive panel built on Radix Dialog, closed by default and invisible to the listener until the facilitator opens it by the vertical tab.
- The **session list** (`ui/organisms/session-list`, ENG-222) is the dashboard's list of sessions (PRD v2 §7.2): a `<ul aria-label="sessões">` of white cards, each showing a status label ("em progresso"/"concluída" via `data-status`), the story title, a slug · project · last-modified meta line, a progress glance built from `StepperStation` molecules, and ONE primary action ("Retomar" or "Abrir"). A facilitator surface — normal density, digits allowed.
- The **artifact cards** (`ui/organisms/artifact-cards`, ENG-222) are the three conclusion document cards (PRD §8.8 / redesign §6.7): three `DocumentCard` molecules whose titles/descriptions are contract-level copy (now dictionary values, @/ui/i18n), keyed by the **export filename, which is never translated**, plus the celebration chip once all three are downloaded. Digit-free per §9.2.
- All of these are presentational — props in, events out. Unlike @/ui/atoms and @/ui/molecules, organisms may import @/domain **types and pure helpers**, may pull their copy from @/ui/i18n (`useTranslation`, ENG-279) and may hold interaction state; they still never import adapters.
- The **session list** (`ui/organisms/session-list`, ENG-222; redesigned to the Shemá v2 prototype in ENG-278) is the dashboard's grid of sessions (PRD v2 §7.2): a `<ul aria-label="sessões">` of story cards, each with a **bead-thumbnail cover** (the §7.2 glanceable progress: `Pearl` atoms lit in proportion to the saved step), the story title, a `slug · project` line, a status label ("Em andamento"/"Concluída", mirrored on `data-status`), "Editado <última modificação>", and ONE primary action ("Retomar" or "Abrir"). An optional `onNew` prepends the dashed "Comece uma nova história" card, which doubles as the empty state. A facilitator surface — normal density, digits allowed.
- The **artifact cards** (`ui/organisms/artifact-cards`, ENG-222) are the three conclusion document cards (PRD §8.8 / redesign §6.7): fixed contractual content (the export filenames plus their human-language PT-BR titles and descriptions, verbatim from the prototypes) composed from three `DocumentCard` molecules, plus the celebration chip "documentos salvos — nada saiu deste computador" once all three are downloaded. Digit-free per §9.2.
- All of these are presentational — props in, events out. Unlike @/ui/atoms and @/ui/molecules, organisms may import @/domain **types and pure helpers** and hold interaction state; they still never import adapters.
- **The barrel @/ui/organisms/index.ts exports only some organisms** (necklace, conversation stage, storyteller guide, seam modal). Connection gate, tutorial popup, Triagem picker, coverage drawer, session list and artifact cards are deliberately NOT exported: editing the barrel was outside their issues' scope (precedent set by ConnectionGate/ENG-224). Consumers import them by direct path, or the consuming station issue (e.g. the Triagem station, ENG-236) adds the export.

### How it fits into the larger codebase

- Sits between @/ui/molecules and the wiring layers (@/ui/pages, @/ui/templates, @/ui/app). Consumers import from the barrel where an export exists, by direct path otherwise; sibling organisms always import each other by direct path (never the barrel) to avoid cycles — the same rule atoms and molecules follow.
- The dependency direction is enforced mechanically by @/.dependency-cruiser.cjs, not convention: organisms may import @/domain (types and pure helpers), @/ui/tokens (`PaletteEntry`, `scenePalette`) and the @/ui/atoms / @/ui/molecules barrels; the ban on adapter imports is a required CI check.
- **Selection decisions stay in the domain.** The necklace reports a raw bead index through callbacks (`onBeadPointerDown`, `onEdgeHover`, `onHeadTap`) and never decides what a click means — the click model lives in @/domain/selection.ts. Likewise the Triagem picker never mutates triagem state: it only emits the contract-ready values (`onConfirm(kind, confidence)` / `onNoneFit()`) for the station to feed into @/domain/triagem.ts, and the coverage drawer only displays a `Coverage` computed by @/domain/coverage.ts.
- **Organisms are where presentational tokens become contract values.** The ConfidenceTrio molecule emits `certeza`/`quase`/`duvida`; the map to the stored `alta`/`média`/`baixa` (@/domain `Confidence`) lives in the Triagem picker, so no page ever converts. English `scene_kind` values pass through untouched — the **display** label comes from `sceneKindLabel` (@/ui/i18n/scene-kind-label.ts), which resolves `skShort` (PT) or `skEnShort` (EN) from @/domain/scene-kinds.ts. The stored value is the contract and never follows the UI language.
- **The seam modal is the interpreter surface for the effects-as-data `BorderOffer`** from @/domain/seam.ts (see @/domain/docs.md): `classifyBorderMove` returns the offer as a pure value and the modal renders EXACTLY the option set that value encodes — `offer.kind` picks the variant (simple / escalation / two-productive) and `offer.canMove` gates "Mover mesmo assim". The modal never computes thresholds; the station will wire the three callbacks back into `moveBorder`/`reanchorFrase`/back-to-Triagem.
- **Radix is used headlessly** (scoped packages: `@radix-ui/react-dialog` — introduced by the seam modal/ENG-228, reused by the coverage drawer — and `@radix-ui/react-popover` for the tutorial popup): behavior/ARIA primitives only (focus trap, ESC, `aria-modal`, scroll lock, anchor positioning); all styling stays the plain-CSS `.cds-*` convention described in @/ui/docs.md.
- `Span` (the `{ s, e }` bead range) is the shared coordinate between the necklace and @/domain — segments, selection, the active-scene window and locked-end beads all arrive as bead indices, matching the "bead indices are the universal coordinate" invariant in @/domain.
- The session list and the artifact cards are **shared building blocks for two pages**: the Dashboard station (@/ui/pages/dashboard) and the Export station (@/ui/pages/export) both compose them; downloads of a concluded session on the dashboard reuse `ArtifactCards`, never a second card variant — which is why the prototype's kebab-menu download was not ported (reconciliation table in @/ui/pages/dashboard/docs.md).
- **The session card takes its progress pre-chewed, never raw session state**: `SessionCardData` carries `progress` (0..1), `progressLabel` (the accessible name of the cover) and an already-formatted `lastModified` string — the organism does no date and no step arithmetic, @/ui/pages/dashboard derives both (`formatWhen`, `progressOf`). This replaced `SessionStationGlance`, a hand-copy of the shell's `StepperStationView` shape that existed only because organisms may not import the wiring layer (@/ui/app); the bead cover needs no shell type at all, so the mirror is gone.

```
ui/tokens ──▶ ui/atoms ──▶ ui/molecules ──▶ ui/organisms ──▶ templates/pages/app
(PaletteEntry) (Pearl…)     (rows, cards)    (this folder)     (adapter wiring +
                                                                domain decisions)
```

### Core Implementation

- **Geometry is a pure port** in @/ui/organisms/necklace/geometry.ts — a 1:1 translation of the reference `renderCord`/`drawBand`/`beadAtXY` (see @/docs/reference/index.html). It has zero DOM/framework imports: `resolveWindow` (active scene ± `max(3, round(2/beadSec))` margin, or the whole story when no scene is active), `beadsPerRow` (from container width), `beadPosition` (absolute left/top of a bead), `beadAtXY` (pointer coords → clamped bead index), `bandRects` (one rectangle per row when a band wraps across the row break), `cordRects` (the thin cord line drawn behind each row, port of the Protótipo's `_rowStyle`: one 2px rect per row, spanning bead-center to bead-center), and `centerOffset` (owner feedback: the necklace stays centered — half the slack between the measured container width and the widest row, `min(count, bpr)` beads, never negative). The pure math is unit-tested independently of the component.
- **`centerOffset` reaches every geometry consumer, not just layout.** `computeField` adds it to every bead's `left`, to the scene/selection band rects and to `cordRects` (via a shared `shift` helper) so the rendered field is always centered; the pointer handler subtracts the SAME offset from `ev.clientX` before calling `beadAtXY`, so hit-testing stays correct against the shifted beads. Getting either side wrong (adding it to draw but not to hit-testing, or vice versa) desyncs clicks from what's drawn — this is why the browser click tests click the CENTER of each bead's `[data-idx]` element rather than a raw computed coordinate, so they stay correct regardless of the current centering math.
- **Size is a per-station prop, not a constant.** @/ui/organisms/necklace/geometry.ts exports the `Size` shape (`slot`/`bead`/`row`) plus the Protótipo-matched presets `SIZE_L` (bead 26px, the Escutas), `SIZE_SEG` (bead 30px, Segmentação) and `SIZE_EXPORT` (bead 22px, Export) alongside the pre-existing default; all four are re-exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts so a page picks its size with `<Necklace size={SIZE_L} .../>` instead of the organism hardcoding one bead size for every station.
- **Imperative island for playback lighting** (@/docs/architecture.md §6): the bead field is a `React.memo` component that receives ONLY structural props — `playbackHead` is deliberately excluded, so feeding a new head does NOT re-render bead elements. A `useLayoutEffect` toggles a `data-play` attribute (`played`/`head`) on bead wrappers via the container ref. DOM node identity stays stable across head changes, so per-frame updates never pass through React state or reconciliation.
- **One delegated native pointer handler**: a single `pointerdown`/`pointermove`/`pointerleave` listener is attached with `addEventListener` on the container inside a mount-once `useLayoutEffect` (NOT React's `onPointerDown`, which delegates at the document root). Pointer → bead is pure geometry (`beadAtXY` over `getBoundingClientRect`), clamped to the render window.
- **Ref-mirror for live props**: because the listeners mount once, they read the latest props through `ixRef`, written inside a layout effect (NOT during render — the `react-hooks/refs` rule forbids ref mutation in render). This keeps the handlers current without re-attaching them.
- **Head-tap precedence** (redesign §6.2, "the glowing head pauses"): when the tapped bead equals the glowing `playbackHead`, `onHeadTap` fires instead of `onBeadPointerDown`.
- The `window` prop is the ACTIVE SCENE span; the organism derives the render window internally, dims the margin beads (`state='dim'`) and draws the dashed scene-band over the scene. `transportOnly` (Escuta/review) suppresses all selection affordances.
- **The conversation stage is a props-driven state machine** (@/ui/organisms/conversation-stage): a full-bleed deep-olive stage with the @/ui/organisms/storyteller-guide on the left and, on the right, the scripted question via the `QuestionCard` molecule (Merriweather text + wordless facilitator role marker). Realigned to the Protótipo: the "Ouvir a pergunta" button moved OUT of `QuestionCard` to sit directly under the guide figure (rendered by this organism, not the molecule, when `onSpeakQuestion` is supplied) and the page that mounts this organism is expected to paint no background of its own — the stage itself is the full-bleed surface (`.cds-conversation-stage`), matching `app.css`'s `:has(.cds-conversation-stage)` dark-chrome selector. A 46-bar `WaveformBar` row renders ALWAYS (flat bars at rest, live-driven by `levels` while `recorderState==='recording'`), and the round mic control is a single 84px button that toggles between the mic glyph and a filled stop-square glyph depending on `recorderState` (`idle`/`recording`) — its `aria-label` still names record vs. stop. `recorded` swaps in "ouvir"/"de novo" small ghost buttons, emitting `onRecord`/`onStop`/`onPlay`/`onRerecord`. Prev/next nav (`onPrev`/`onNext`) rounds it out. No MediaRecorder lives here — that state machine lives in the adapters layer and is wired by the station page (ENG-249). **The interview is voice-only (owner decision, design parity):** the organism dropped its `typedAnswer` prop entirely — the "a facilitadora pode escrever depois — nunca por você" hint is now a permanent line rather than one gated on that prop being supplied, and there is no `textarea` on this stage; typing moved to @/ui/pages/relatorio's editable report cards (§8.7), after the conversation ends.
- **The question thread is windowed, not the full script.** `BeadRow` is now fed at most `THREAD_WINDOW` (23) `BeadCell`s centered on `progress.current`, sliding the window as the interview advances instead of rendering all `progress.total` beads — the Protótipo itself shows only 11, while the real interview script (@/domain/mapping.ts `questionSequence`) can reach 41 questions, which would otherwise overflow the stage width with no scroll (§9.2 forbids a scrollable/countable listener surface). The current bead renders larger (22px vs 15px) instead of using a distinct playhead ring.
- **The seam modal is purely presentational and controlled** (@/ui/organisms/seam-modal): props are the `BorderOffer | null` (type-only @/domain import; `null` renders nothing), the scene and its immediate neighbor as `{ span, tint }` cord data (`SeamCordSide`; `null` neighbor = the border only stretches the scene), plus the outcome callbacks `onMove`/`onReanchor`/`onGoTriagem`. A small pure strip model windows a few beads around the seam, clamps to the given spans and zones each bead as scene / overshoot / neighbor; the overshoot beads get a light core over the scene tint and the cream selection halo from prototype 1g.
- **The Radix policy** (decided 2026-07-09; applied by the seam modal's and the coverage drawer's `@radix-ui/react-dialog` and the tutorial popup's `@radix-ui/react-popover`): Radix contributes BEHAVIOR only — focus trap, ESC, dismissal, anchor positioning — while every visual stays 100% Shemá tokens via the usual `.cds-*` CSS. In the seam modal, ESC and overlay click both funnel through Radix `onOpenChange(false)` → `onReanchor`, the safe default that only discards the selection (§9.5: guide, never punish).
- **The tutorial popup deliberately skips `Popover.Portal`**: the content renders inline so it stays INSIDE the shell's `.cds-addons-layer` (@/ui/app/addons-layer.tsx) instead of escaping to `document.body` — the requirement is overlay-layer chrome that never lands in the station's `main`. Radix's internal Popper still positions the card `position: fixed` relative to the trigger even without a portal. It auto-opens on mount WITHOUT stealing focus (`onOpenAutoFocus` prevented — it is non-modal status chrome), and dismissal has two levels: X/ESC/click-outside hide it while a session stays loaded (component `useState`; the addon stays mounted across stations, so the choice survives station changes — but leaving to the dashboard unmounts the popup, and another session re-offers the tips), while "Não mostrar de novo" persists via localStorage. The "?" trigger remains as the re-entry route in both cases — dismissing never costs the information. An unknown station key renders nothing, and the tips carry zero digits (same §9.2 sweep as the other listener-adjacent surfaces).
- **Seam-slide preview is static-first**: both markers — "borda de hoje" (dashed) and "borda nova" (ember-glow) — always render, so the before/after states are visible without motion; the slide animation of the new border lives only inside `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`, CSS-source-asserted in tests exactly like the necklace. Marker order mirrors the crossing side (`crossEnd` vs `crossStart`).
- **The "thread of beads" progress row** at the bottom reuses the `BeadRow` molecule — one Pearl per question, `head` for `progress.current`, `lit` for each answered index, `unplayed` otherwise. Per PRD §9.2 it shows zero digits: the current bead beats the answered state, and no counter/total ever renders as text.
- **The storyteller guide introduces the component-level variant-glob upgrade pattern** (@/docs/architecture.md §4). Its `index.tsx` reads `import.meta.glob('./variants/*.tsx', { eager: true })` and the pure `pickVariantPath()` helper in @/ui/organisms/storyteller-guide/variant.ts selects a variant by file basename — preferring `animated` over `static`. ENG-221 shipped `variants/static.tsx` (a warm human figure — head, face, gentle smile — drawn in SVG/CSS, deliberately NOT abstract/geometric); ENG-232 added `variants/animated.tsx` (the same figure, now with a bob/blink idle loop and a `data-speaking`-gated lip-sync) WITHOUT editing any existing file — the glob now picks it over the static, proving the add-a-file upgrade. This mirrors the shell's three `import.meta.glob` add-a-file registries, scoped inside a single component. The shared `GuideVariantProps` (`speaking`, `size`) let the static variant ignore `speaking` while the animated one drives lip-sync from it. All of the animated variant's motion (`bob`/`blink`/`talk` keyframes) lives behind `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`, so `reduce` leaves the same static pose (§4.5); its test sits in `variants/__tests__/` so the `./variants/*.tsx` glob never imports it as a variant.
- **The Triagem picker is a two-stage machine.** Stage 1 is a single grid: `tier === 'comum'` kinds from `SCENE_KINDS` under "Mais comuns", a disclosure ("Ver todos os tipos por tema") that appends every kind grouped into six display themes, the dashed none-fit card, and a filter that folds accents/case (NFD strip) and matches three things — the PT-BR label, the currently displayed label (PT or EN) and the English contract value — so filtering never degrades by switching language. Picking a kind swaps the entire grid for stage 2: the chosen chip (Pearl + PT label, EN in `title`), a "trocar tipo" escape hatch back to stage 1, and the ConfidenceTrio — choosing there only SELECTS (local state, real `aria-checked`); the contract emission happens on the explicit "Confirmar" button revealed after the first choice, because the trio is a standard APG radiogroup where arrow keys move-and-select, and select-equals-commit would let keyboard exploration commit the triage.
- **Theme membership is display-only and lives in the picker**, deliberately NOT in @/domain/scene-kinds.ts (that list is generated and never hand-edited). Each theme carries a **stable slug id** (used as its `data-theme`) separate from its translated visible heading — since ENG-279 the label is no longer a usable identifier. Theme tints are the leading entries of `scenePalette` from @/ui/tokens; the fallback for a kind outside every theme is `scenePalette[5]` (a dead path today — every kind is mapped).
- **The coverage drawer is a thin formatter over domain data**: it receives a ready `Coverage` (@/domain/coverage.ts), renders one mono row per kind that has any count (the English contract value + firme/hesitante/alvo counts) plus the absence-candidate chips, whose labels resolve through `sceneKindLabel` (@/ui/i18n). The "1–2" target display for the rare (ALTA) tier is the drawer's presentation decision — domain supplies a single numeric target. Trigger, portal, overlay, focus trap and dismissal (ESC, outside tap, ×) are all Radix Dialog.

### Things to Know

- **Listener-minimalism guards** (PRD v2 §9.2): @/ui/organisms/necklace/minimalism.test.tsx and @/ui/organisms/triagem-picker/minimalism.test.tsx assert no digit ever renders as visible text, `aria-label`, `title` or `placeholder` on these listener-facing surfaces — hence copy like "Ver todos os tipos por tema" instead of "os 27 tipos". The coverage drawer is exempt by design: it is a facilitator-density surface where digits are allowed, but it must stay closed by default and fully absent from the document until opened.
- **Test split by file suffix** (@/vitest.config.ts): `*.test.tsx` runs in jsdom (`necklace.test.tsx` asserts the render vocabulary; `geometry.test.ts` covers the pure math), while `*.browser.test.tsx` runs in real Chromium — required here because jsdom has zeroed layout and no real hit-testing. There is **no `vitest-browser-react`** in the repo: browser tests drive the component with raw `createRoot`/`flushSync` and native `dispatchEvent(new PointerEvent(...))` for coordinate hit-testing.
- The 280 ms hover-edge dwell (a single edge preview fires only after the pointer rests near a selection edge) is tested with `vi.useFakeTimers()` + `advanceTimersByTimeAsync`; the single-listener claim is verified by spying on `addEventListener`. Touch pointers skip hover entirely.
- `ResizeObserver` is feature-detected — absent in jsdom, where the one synchronous width measure suffices; in the browser it re-measures on resize.
- Pearl (@/ui/atoms) stays non-clickable: the organism owns all pointer handling, matching the necklace performance pattern described in @/ui/docs.md. The scene-end square, dim margin and selection edge-ring are all Pearl `data-*` states driven from `computeField`.
- **The recorder-flow interaction test is mandatory** and lives in `conversation-stage.browser.test.tsx` (real Chromium, raw `createRoot`/`flushSync` + native click), because the conversation recorder is one of CLAUDE.md's three interaction-critical surfaces; a companion `conversation-stage.test.tsx` runs in jsdom for the structural assertions. The seam modal follows the same split — its focus trap is only provable in Chromium (jsdom has no native Tab navigation), so its mandatory interaction test lives in the browser suite too.
- **The seam modal deliberately does NOT render the domain's `question`/`warning` strings**: those carry digits (the WHY_DELTA copy in `BORDER_COPY`, @/domain/seam.ts), and this is a listener-facing surface (§9.2). It shows the redesign headline, the PRD button copy and the `simple`-only consequence line, all from @/ui/i18n; `delta`/`thr` stay internal. A side effect worth knowing: because it renders no domain copy, the seam modal is one of the few surfaces that is **fully** translated — the domain error/gate copy elsewhere stays PT-BR. Each action carries a stable `key` (`move`/`reanchor`/…) because the translated label can no longer identify the focus-target button. A minimalism test sweeps every offer variant for leaked digits in text, `aria-label` and `title`.
- **Radix Dialog conventions** (set by the seam modal, continued by the coverage drawer): `Dialog.Title` is mandatory (Radix warns/errors without it); with no `Dialog.Description`, pass `aria-describedby={undefined}` on `Dialog.Content` so it doesn't reference a nonexistent id; slide/fade animations live ONLY inside the `prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference` guard — without them Radix unmounts the panel instantly, which is the correct base behavior, so reduced-motion needs no extra handling. `Dialog.Portal` renders to `document.body`, so tests query via `screen`/`document`, never the render container. The modal Dialog defers pointer-down-outside dismissal to the subsequent `click` event (`deferPointerDownOutside`), so outside-dismiss tests must fire pointerdown AND click — and the outside listener attaches in a `setTimeout(0)`, so give the tick first (same quirk recorded in @/ui/organisms/coverage-drawer/coverage-drawer.test.tsx).
- **Roving tabindex without refs:** `KindCard` (@/ui/molecules) does not forward a ref, so the picker's keyboard handling queries `[role="radio"]` in DOM order via `querySelectorAll` on the group container, finds `document.activeElement` among them and focuses the next one. Exactly one card is `tabbable` at a time (the `tabbable` prop), tracked as a focused index clamped when the radio count changes (filter/disclosure).
- **The picker is one single radiogroup** spanning commons + theme sections + the none-fit card, with theme headings as purely visual labels: nesting `role=group` inside a `radiogroup` is not sanctioned by ARIA (a radiogroup's required owned children are radios). Arrow keys move focus WITHOUT selecting (the APG toolbar variant, not the standard radio move-and-select) — selecting reveals the confidence step and destroys the grid, so select-on-arrow would be destructive.
- **Context override, not a new variant**: inside the olive panel the Button atom's ghost variant (olive-on-cream by default) is recolored to a cream outline by a CSS rule scoped under `.cds-seam-modal` — a look-layer override per prototype 1g, leaving the @/ui/atoms Button untouched.
- **prefers-reduced-motion is honored throughout**: every decorative animation in this folder (idle mic pulse, seam-slide, drawer slide/fade, picker motion) sits under the `no-preference` guard, asserted in tests via `?raw` CSS imports, and the static storyteller guide carries no motion at all.
- **Sibling organisms import each other by direct path, never the barrel** — the conversation stage pulls the storyteller guide from `../storyteller-guide` (its folder `index.tsx` entry), keeping the variant-glob resolution encapsulated and avoiding a cycle through @/ui/organisms/index.ts.
- **Shell-only organisms stay out of the barrel**: the connection gate and the tutorial popup are deliberately NOT exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts — only @/ui/app consumes them, by direct path. Adding them to the barrel would invite station pages to mount shell chrome.
- **First localStorage use in the repo** is the tutorial popup's permanent dismiss: a namespaced, versioned key (`colar-de-sons:tutorial:dismissed:v1`) with every get/set wrapped in try/catch and silent degradation (blocked storage → the dismissal only lasts the session, the app never breaks). New persistent-preference keys should follow this precedent; rationale in @/ui/organisms/tutorial-popup/README.md.
- **Radix Popover's Popper needs `ResizeObserver`**, which jsdom lacks — the tutorial-popup tests stub a no-op class in `beforeAll`. Unlike the seam modal (portal to body, focus trap only provable in Chromium), the popup is fully testable in jsdom because it renders inline and traps no focus.
- **Each session card exposes exactly one interactive element**: a native button named by composition — the visible verb ("Retomar"/"Abrir") plus a visually-hidden span carrying the story title, so screen readers hear "Retomar/Abrir <título>". The whole card is never clickable and no interactives nest — clicking chooses `onResume` for in-progress sessions and `onOpen` for concluded ones. This is also why the status label is a plain `<span>` with a dot and NOT the `Chip` atom (@/ui/atoms), which renders a `<button>`: a status is not an action. The dashed new-story card is the one card that IS a button, and it only exists when `onNew` is supplied.
- **The card cover is a decorative miniature, not a real necklace**: a fixed 22-bead field (two rows of 11) whose lit count is `round(progress × 22)` — clamped to 0..1 — and whose tints cycle @/ui/tokens `scenePalette` every four beads. That grouping is visual rhythm only; it has nothing to do with the story's actual bead grid or scene spans (@/domain). It renders as ONE `role="img"` named by `progressLabel`, so the glance is announced as a single sentence rather than 22 anonymous beads, and no card ever nests a list.
