# Noridoc: ui/organisms

Path: @/ui/organisms

### Overview

- The third presentational layer of the Shem√° design system: stateful compositions that consume @/domain state via props/hooks and own the interaction-critical surfaces the listener touches. This folder ships the **necklace**, the **conversation stage**, the **storyteller guide** and the **seam modal** (exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts) plus two shell-only organisms ‚Äî the **connection gate** and the **tutorial popup** ‚Äî that stay out of the barrel and are consumed by @/ui/app via direct path.
- The **necklace** (`ui/organisms/necklace`, ENG-220) is the hero "colar" (redesign ¬ß6) that renders the whole story as a wrapped field of beads: the full bead-state vocabulary (palette-tinted segment beads, square end beads for locked scene ends, a soft selection band with emphasized edge beads, a dim + dashed scene-band over the active-scene window in Segmenta√ß√£o) plus its own click model, hover-edge preview and 60 fps playback lighting.
- The **conversation stage** (`ui/organisms/conversation-stage`, ENG-221) is the Mapeamento (Conversa) surface (redesign ¬ß6.6), and the **storyteller guide** (`ui/organisms/storyteller-guide`, ENG-221) is the warm human figure it seats on the left (¬ß6.6, ¬ß9.7). Both are purely presentational ‚Äî props in, callbacks out.
- The **connection gate** (`ui/organisms/connection-gate`, ENG-224) is the online-only editing shield (PRD ¬ß7.3/¬ß13): `ConnectionGate({ online, children })` renders its children always mounted and, when offline, adds a PT-BR `role=status` banner plus a blocking overlay over the editing surface. It is a pure organism (no adapters ‚Äî `online` arrives resolved by the shell), and is deliberately NOT exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts (the barrel was out of ENG-224's scope); @/ui/app imports it by direct path.
- The **seam modal** (`ui/organisms/seam-modal`, ENG-228) is the border-crossing dialog (PRD v2 ¬ß8.6; redesign ¬ß6.5, prototype "1g ‚Äî a costura desliza"): a full-olive panel showing a small stretch of the cord with today's border and the proposed new one, offering exactly the choices the domain classified. The Segmenta√ß√£o station will open it when a phrase selection crosses the scene border (wiring lands with ENG-237).
- The **tutorial popup** (`ui/organisms/tutorial-popup`, ENG-231) is the facilitator's per-station tips chrome (PRD v2 ¬ß7.5): a cream card of one short PT-BR tip per station, anchored to a round "?" trigger in the shell's bottom-right overlay layer. It is mounted only by the shell addon @/ui/app/addons/tutorial.tsx (never by a station page); its dismissal semantics and localStorage key are documented in @/ui/organisms/tutorial-popup/README.md.
- Unlike @/ui/atoms and @/ui/molecules, organisms are allowed to import @/domain **types** and hold interaction state; they still never import adapters.

### How it fits into the larger codebase

- Sits between @/ui/molecules and the wiring layers (@/ui/pages, @/ui/templates, @/ui/app). Consumers import from the public barrel @/ui/organisms/index.ts; sibling organisms import each other by direct path (never the barrel) to avoid cycles ‚Äî the same rule atoms and molecules follow.
- The dependency direction is enforced mechanically by @/.dependency-cruiser.cjs, not convention: organisms may import @/domain (types only), @/ui/tokens (`PaletteEntry`) and the @/ui/atoms / @/ui/molecules barrels; the ban on adapter imports is a required CI check.
- **Selection decisions stay in the domain.** The necklace reports a raw bead index through callbacks (`onBeadPointerDown`, `onEdgeHover`, `onHeadTap`) and never decides what a click means ‚Äî the click model that turns a bead into a selection lives in @/domain/selection.ts. The organism is the pointer-to-index transducer plus the renderer; the page wires the domain decision back in as new props.
- `Span` (the `{ s, e }` bead range) is the shared coordinate between this organism and @/domain ‚Äî segments, selection, the active-scene window and locked-end beads all arrive as bead indices, matching the "bead indices are the universal coordinate" invariant in @/domain.
- **The seam modal is the interpreter surface for the effects-as-data `BorderOffer`** from @/domain/seam.ts (see @/domain/docs.md): `classifyBorderMove` returns the offer as a pure value and the modal renders EXACTLY the option set that value encodes ‚Äî `offer.kind` picks the variant (simple / escalation / two-productive) and `offer.canMove` gates "Mover mesmo assim". The modal never computes thresholds; the station will wire the three callbacks back into `moveBorder`/`reanchorFrase`/back-to-Triagem.

### Core Implementation

- **Geometry is a pure port** in @/ui/organisms/necklace/geometry.ts ‚Äî a 1:1 translation of the reference `renderCord`/`drawBand`/`beadAtXY` (see @/docs/reference/index.html). It has zero DOM/framework imports: `resolveWindow` (active scene ¬± `max(3, round(2/beadSec))` margin, or the whole story when no scene is active), `beadsPerRow` (from container width), `beadPosition` (absolute left/top of a bead), `beadAtXY` (pointer coords ‚Üí clamped bead index), and `bandRects` (one rectangle per row when a band wraps across the row break). The pure math is unit-tested independently of the component.
- **Imperative island for playback lighting** (@/docs/architecture.md ¬ß6): the bead field is a `React.memo` component that receives ONLY structural props ‚Äî `playbackHead` is deliberately excluded, so feeding a new head does NOT re-render bead elements. A `useLayoutEffect` toggles a `data-play` attribute (`played`/`head`) on bead wrappers via the container ref. DOM node identity stays stable across head changes, so per-frame updates never pass through React state or reconciliation.
- **One delegated native pointer handler**: a single `pointerdown`/`pointermove`/`pointerleave` listener is attached with `addEventListener` on the container inside a mount-once `useLayoutEffect` (NOT React's `onPointerDown`, which delegates at the document root). Pointer ‚Üí bead is pure geometry (`beadAtXY` over `getBoundingClientRect`), clamped to the render window.
- **Ref-mirror for live props**: because the listeners mount once, they read the latest props through `ixRef`, written inside a layout effect (NOT during render ‚Äî the `react-hooks/refs` rule forbids ref mutation in render). This keeps the handlers current without re-attaching them.
- **Head-tap precedence** (redesign ¬ß6.2, "the glowing head pauses"): when the tapped bead equals the glowing `playbackHead`, `onHeadTap` fires instead of `onBeadPointerDown`.
- The `window` prop is the ACTIVE SCENE span; the organism derives the render window internally, dims the margin beads (`state='dim'`) and draws the dashed scene-band over the scene. `transportOnly` (Escuta/review) suppresses all selection affordances.
- **The conversation stage is a props-driven state machine** (@/ui/organisms/conversation-stage): a full-bleed deep-olive stage with the @/ui/organisms/storyteller-guide on the left and, on the right, the scripted question via the `QuestionCard` molecule (Merriweather text + wordless facilitator role marker + the conditional "Ouvir a pergunta" button, shown only when `onSpeakQuestion` is supplied). The recorder area switches on `recorderState` (`idle` ‚Üí mic button / `recording` ‚Üí live `WaveformBar` waveform driven by the `levels` prop + "Parar" / `recorded` ‚Üí "ouvir" + "de novo"), emitting `onRecord`/`onStop`/`onPlay`/`onRerecord`. An optional `typedAnswer` channel and prev/next nav (`onPrev`/`onNext`) round it out. No MediaRecorder lives here ‚Äî that state machine lives in the adapters layer and is wired by the station page (ENG-249).
- **The seam modal is purely presentational and controlled** (@/ui/organisms/seam-modal): props are the `BorderOffer | null` (type-only @/domain import; `null` renders nothing), the scene and its immediate neighbor as `{ span, tint }` cord data (`SeamCordSide`; `null` neighbor = the border only stretches the scene), plus the outcome callbacks `onMove`/`onReanchor`/`onGoTriagem`. A small pure strip model windows a few beads around the seam, clamps to the given spans and zones each bead as scene / overshoot / neighbor; the overshoot beads get a light core over the scene tint and the cream selection halo from prototype 1g.
- **The Radix policy** (decided 2026-07-09; applied by the seam modal's `@radix-ui/react-dialog` and the tutorial popup's `@radix-ui/react-popover`): Radix contributes BEHAVIOR only ‚Äî focus trap, ESC, dismissal, anchor positioning ‚Äî while every visual stays 100% Shem√° tokens via the usual `.cds-*` CSS. In the seam modal, ESC and overlay click both funnel through Radix `onOpenChange(false)` ‚Üí `onReanchor`, the safe default that only discards the selection (¬ß9.5: guide, never punish).
- **The tutorial popup deliberately skips `Popover.Portal`**: the content renders inline so it stays INSIDE the shell's `.cds-addons-layer` (@/ui/app/addons-layer.tsx) instead of escaping to `document.body` ‚Äî the requirement is overlay-layer chrome that never lands in the station's `main`. Radix's internal Popper still positions the card `position: fixed` relative to the trigger even without a portal. It auto-opens on mount WITHOUT stealing focus (`onOpenAutoFocus` prevented ‚Äî it is non-modal status chrome), and dismissal has two levels: X/ESC/click-outside hide it while a session stays loaded (component `useState`; the addon stays mounted across stations, so the choice survives station changes ‚Äî but leaving to the dashboard unmounts the popup, and another session re-offers the tips), while "N√£o mostrar de novo" persists via localStorage. The "?" trigger remains as the re-entry route in both cases ‚Äî dismissing never costs the information. An unknown station key renders nothing, and the tips carry zero digits (same ¬ß9.2 sweep as the other listener-adjacent surfaces).
- **Seam-slide preview is static-first**: both markers ‚Äî "borda de hoje" (dashed) and "borda nova" (ember-glow) ‚Äî always render, so the before/after states are visible without motion; the slide animation of the new border lives only inside `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`, CSS-source-asserted in tests exactly like the necklace. Marker order mirrors the crossing side (`crossEnd` vs `crossStart`).
- **The "thread of beads" progress row** at the bottom reuses the `BeadRow` molecule ‚Äî one Pearl per question, `head` for `progress.current`, `lit` for each answered index, `unplayed` otherwise. Per PRD ¬ß9.2 it shows zero digits: the current bead beats the answered state, and no counter/total ever renders as text.
- **The storyteller guide introduces the component-level variant-glob upgrade pattern** (@/docs/architecture.md ¬ß4). Its `index.tsx` reads `import.meta.glob('./variants/*.tsx', { eager: true })` and the pure `pickVariantPath()` helper in @/ui/organisms/storyteller-guide/variant.ts selects a variant by file basename ‚Äî preferring `animated` over `static`. This issue ships only `variants/static.tsx` (a warm human figure ‚Äî head, face, gentle smile ‚Äî drawn in SVG/CSS, deliberately NOT abstract/geometric); ENG-232 will add `variants/animated.tsx` (bob/blink/lip-sync) WITHOUT editing any existing file. This mirrors the shell's three `import.meta.glob` add-a-file registries, scoped inside a single component. The shared `GuideVariantProps` (`speaking`, `size`) let the static variant ignore `speaking` while the future animated one drives lip-sync from it.

### Things to Know

- @/ui/organisms/necklace/minimalism.test.tsx is the layer-level guard for PRD v2 ¬ß9.2 listener minimalism, mirroring the atoms/molecules guards: the rendered necklace may show no digit as visible text, `aria-label` or `title`. IDs and counters never appear on this listener-facing surface.
- **Test split by file suffix** (@/vitest.config.ts): `*.test.tsx` runs in jsdom (`necklace.test.tsx` asserts the render vocabulary; `geometry.test.ts` covers the pure math), while `*.browser.test.tsx` runs in real Chromium ‚Äî required here because jsdom has zeroed layout and no real hit-testing. There is **no `vitest-browser-react`** in the repo: browser tests drive the component with raw `createRoot`/`flushSync` and native `dispatchEvent(new PointerEvent(...))` for coordinate hit-testing.
- The 280 ms hover-edge dwell (a single edge preview fires only after the pointer rests near a selection edge) is tested with `vi.useFakeTimers()` + `advanceTimersByTimeAsync`; the single-listener claim is verified by spying on `addEventListener`. Touch pointers skip hover entirely.
- `ResizeObserver` is feature-detected ‚Äî absent in jsdom, where the one synchronous width measure suffices; in the browser it re-measures on resize.
- Pearl (@/ui/atoms) stays non-clickable: the organism owns all pointer handling, matching the necklace performance pattern described in @/ui/docs.md. The scene-end square, dim margin and selection edge-ring are all Pearl `data-*` states driven from `computeField`.
- **The recorder-flow interaction test is mandatory** and lives in `conversation-stage.browser.test.tsx` (real Chromium, raw `createRoot`/`flushSync` + native click), because the conversation recorder is one of CLAUDE.md's three interaction-critical surfaces; a companion `conversation-stage.test.tsx` runs in jsdom for the structural assertions. The seam modal follows the same split ‚Äî its focus trap is only provable in Chromium (jsdom has no native Tab navigation), so its mandatory interaction test lives in the browser suite too.
- **The seam modal deliberately does NOT render the domain's `question`/`warning` strings**: those carry digits (the WHY_DELTA copy in `BORDER_COPY`, @/domain/seam.ts), and this is a listener-facing surface (¬ß9.2). It shows the redesign headline "A frase passou da borda da cena.", the PRD button copy as local constants, and the consequence line only for the `simple` variant; `delta`/`thr` stay internal. A minimalism test sweeps every offer variant for leaked digits in text, `aria-label` and `title`.
- **Radix Dialog gotchas recorded for future tests**: `Dialog.Portal` renders to `document.body`, so tests query via `screen`/`document`, never the render container. The modal Dialog defers pointer-down-outside dismissal to the subsequent `click` event (`deferPointerDownOutside`), so outside-dismiss tests must fire pointerdown AND click ‚Äî and the outside listener attaches in a `setTimeout(0)`, so give the tick first.
- **Context override, not a new variant**: inside the olive panel the Button atom's ghost variant (olive-on-cream by default) is recolored to a cream outline by a CSS rule scoped under `.cds-seam-modal` ‚Äî a look-layer override per prototype 1g, leaving the @/ui/atoms Button untouched.
- **prefers-reduced-motion is honored throughout**: every decorative animation (the idle mic pulse in the conversation stage, the seam-slide in the seam modal) is gated under `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)`, and the static storyteller guide carries no motion at all.
- **Sibling organisms import each other by direct path, never the barrel** ‚Äî the conversation stage pulls the storyteller guide from `../storyteller-guide` (its folder `index.tsx` entry), keeping the variant-glob resolution encapsulated and avoiding a cycle through @/ui/organisms/index.ts.
- **Shell-only organisms stay out of the barrel**: the connection gate and the tutorial popup are deliberately NOT exported from @/ui/organisms/index.ts ‚Äî only @/ui/app consumes them, by direct path. Adding them to the barrel would invite station pages to mount shell chrome.
- **First localStorage use in the repo** is the tutorial popup's permanent dismiss: a namespaced, versioned key (`colar-de-sons:tutorial:dismissed:v1`) with every get/set wrapped in try/catch and silent degradation (blocked storage ‚Üí the dismissal only lasts the session, the app never breaks). New persistent-preference keys should follow this precedent; rationale in @/ui/organisms/tutorial-popup/README.md.
- **Radix Popover's Popper needs `ResizeObserver`**, which jsdom lacks ‚Äî the tutorial-popup tests stub a no-op class in `beforeAll`. Unlike the seam modal (portal to body, focus trap only provable in Chromium), the popup is fully testable in jsdom because it renders inline and traps no focus.
- **The connection gate only shows the affordance; the actual freeze lives elsewhere.** Its overlay covers the editing surface but keeps children mounted so state is preserved, and the traveling player (@/ui/app/player-slot.tsx) sits in its own layer outside the overlay so playback survives an outage. The mutation block itself is the `apply` no-op in @/ui/state (see @/ui/state/docs.md), fed by the same offline signal.
